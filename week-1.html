Summer Holiday Club 2026 · Ages 4–12

Week One — 27–31 July

A week to make, play and explore.

Our first week of the summer invites children to slow down, head outdoors and rediscover the simple joys of making, playing and exploring together. Set on the edge of the New Forest, with nature all around us, each day carries its own gentle theme — with plenty of room to roam, imagine and simply be a child.

Screen-free and led by our Waldorf-trained team, it's a week to come home tired, happy and full of stories.

The week, day by day
Mon27 JULY

A crafting welcome

We begin gently. The morning is given over to outdoor crafts — working with our hands and natural materials to make something to treasure. In the afternoon there's time for games, imaginative play and the unhurried freedom of free play, following wherever the day leads.

Tue28 JULY

Bushcraft day

A day of bushcraft in the fresh air, right here on site. Children build dens, try their hand at simple whittling, and gather around the fire to make and share stick bread, warm from the flames — a day of practical skills, teamwork and the quiet confidence that comes from doing real things outdoors.

Wed29 JULY

Theatre day

Imagination takes the stage. Together we create outdoor theatre — making puppets, weaving stories and bringing them to life — building towards a show to share by day's end. A day of storytelling, courage and a little bit of magic.

Thu30 JULY

Explorer day

Out into the wider world. With maps in hand, the children set off on nature walks and treasure hunts — looking closely, finding their way, and discovering the small wonders hidden along the path.

Fri31 JULY

Water fun day

We end the week with laughter and splashes. Water slides, home-made model boats, bubbles and obstacle courses make for a joyful, sun-filled finish to our first week together.

Nurturing bright young futures with confidence, creativity and compassion.
