Week One — 27–31 July

A week to make, play and explore.

Our first week of the summer invites children to slow down, head outdoors and rediscover the simple joys of making, playing and exploring together. Set on the edge of the New Forest, with nature all around us, each day carries its own gentle theme — with plenty of room to roam, imagine and simply be a child.

Screen-free and led by our Waldorf-trained team, it's a week to come home tired, happy and full of stories.

The week, day by day
Mon27 JULY

Craft & play day

We begin gently. A morning of outdoor crafts — making land art from natural treasures and weaving dream catchers — alongside imaginative play and plenty of unhurried free play, following wherever the day leads.

Tue28 JULY

Bushcraft day

A day of bushcraft in the fresh air, right here on site. Children build dens, try simple whittling, and gather around the fire for stories and stick bread warm from the flames — practical skills, teamwork and quiet confidence.

Wed29 JULY

Theatre day

Imagination takes the stage. Together we create outdoor theatre — making puppets, weaving stories and building towards a show to share by day's end. A day of storytelling, courage and a little magic.

Thu30 JULY

Explorer day

Out to discover. With maps in hand the children set off on nature walks and treasure hunts — making and solving cryptic clues, tackling obstacles along the way, finding their way and noticing the small wonders on the path.

Fri31 JULY

Water fun day

The week ends outdoors with water slides, home-made model boats, bubbles and obstacle courses — plenty to keep everyone busy and cool in the summer sun.

Nurturing bright young futures with confidence, creativity and compassion.
